Cotton

Cotton is a soft, fluffy staple fiber that grows in a boll, or protective case, around the seeds of the cotton plants of the genus Gossypium in the mallow family Malvaceae. It's one of the most important fiber and cash crops worldwide, used extensively in the textile industry to make a variety of products, including clothing, bedding, and towels.

Cultivation and Growth

  • Climate: Cotton thrives in warm, subtropical climates with plenty of sunlight.
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soils are ideal for cotton cultivation.
  • Planting: Cotton is typically planted in the spring or early summer..
  • GrowthCotton plants grow tall and bushy, producing bolls that contain the cotton fibers.

Associated Diseases

  • Cotton Wilt:

    Is a serious fungal disease that can cause significant damage to cotton crops. It is caused by the fungus Verticillium dahliae or Fusarium oxysporum. These fungi invade the plant's vascular system, blocking the flow of water and nutrients
  • Powdery Mildew:
  • Septoria Leaf Spot: A fungal disease causing dark spots on leaves.
