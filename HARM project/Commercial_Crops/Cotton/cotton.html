Cotton

Cotton is a soft, fluffy fiber that grows around the seeds of the cotton plant. It is one of the most important cash crops used worldwide in the textile industry, for products like clothing, towels, and more.

Cultivation and Growth

  • Climate: Thrives in warm, subtropical climates with plenty of sunlight.
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soils are ideal for cotton cultivation.
  • Planting: Cotton is typically planted in the spring or early summer.
  • Growth: Cotton plants grow tall and bushy, producing bolls that contain cotton fibers.

Associated Diseases

Cotton Wilt

Cotton wilt is caused by the fungi Verticillium dahliae or Fusarium oxysporum. These fungi invade the plant's vascular system, blocking the flow of water and nutrients, causing wilting.

Verticillium Wilt

This disease causes leaf curling and wilting due to fungal infection in the vascular tissues. The plants exhibit stunted growth and discoloration.

Bacterial Blight

Bacterial blight causes lesions on leaves and bolls, leading to defoliation and crop loss. It spreads through water and contaminated seeds.

Anthracnose

A fungal disease that causes dark, sunken lesions on bolls and leaves, leading to premature boll shedding and crop loss.

Root Rot

This disease affects the roots of cotton plants, leading to yellowing of leaves and plant wilting. It is caused by soilborne fungi.

Alternaria Leaf Spot

This fungal disease causes small, circular dark spots on leaves, eventually leading to defoliation and reduced crop yield.
